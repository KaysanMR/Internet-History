The internet's journey is a captivating tale.

It began modestly in the late 1960s with ARPANET—a network connecting research computers. Fast-forward to the '90s, and the World Wide Web emerged, revolutionizing how we access information. The dot-com boom birthed giants like Amazon and eBay. Social media and mobile devices took center stage in the 2000s. Cloud computing, streaming services, and AI defined the 2010s. Today, the internet is our global lifeline, and its future holds exciting possibilities—privacy debates, cybersecurity challenges, and innovations yet to come.
